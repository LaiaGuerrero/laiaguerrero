CASA HERRERA SEMINAR

a sophisticated website for a Carolina Herrera corporate event

Overview

Imagine the Casa Herrera Seminar website as the key to an exclusive beauty experience—seamlessly blending elegance with functionality. This digital experience guides international attendees through a refined registration journey, offering tailored event details, curated local insights, and a VIP-inspired welcome.

My role

I led the design of the Casa Herrera Seminar website, ensuring it reflects the brand requirements and user experience standards. Also, I worked closely with the project manager to make key decisions, and review each step to align with the client's vision.

Impact & Results

→ 30% Faster Registration: Streamlined user flow reduced registration time by 30% compared to last year’s website.

→ Improved Conversion: A more intuitive journey increased the attendance confirmation rate.

→ Enhanced User Experience: Clear local insights and mobile-first design significantly improved usability for international professionals.

The challenge Key decisions Design process Final Design Learnings

THE CHALLENGE

Carolina Herrera’s parent company, Puig, needed a website that would serve as an exclusive digital gateway for their Casa Herrera Seminar, an educational corporate event.

The key goal was to design a mobile-optimized registration website that balanced the brand’s sophisticated identity with practical functionality. Previous event websites suffered from poor mobile usability, unclear communication channels, and inadequate local information for international attendees.

KEY DECISONS

→ Mobile-First Approach: With 68% of last year’s visitors accessing the site via mobile, designing for small screens was non-negotiable.

→ Invitation-Style Registration: Instead of a bland form, we crafted a registration flow that felt like receiving a personal invitation—complete with a “Welcome Pass” and tailored local recommendations.

→ Elegant Minimalism: Balancing Carolina Herrera’s minimalist elegance with robust functionality ensured that every visual element—from typography to interactive cues—reinforced a luxurious yet user-friendly experience.

DESIGN PROCESS

1. Understand

Conducted surveys and interviews with 20 potential users to uncover pain points such as mobile usability issues and the need for clear, accessible event information.

2. Define

Immersed in Carolina Herrera’s aesthetic guidelines, we identified key design elements that echoed the brand’s sophistication and the event venue’s architectural beauty.

3. Ideate

Developed user personas and mapped out the registration journey as a “digital key” to the event. Created sketch wireframes and iterated through low- and high-fidelity prototypes in Figma.

4. Iterate

Engaged 10 users in one-on-one sessions to refine the navigation flow and ensure the design resonated across devices.

FINAL DESIGN

After incorporating user feedback and refining the prototype, I prepared the final design and all necessary guidelines, assets, and documentation to ensure a smooth handover. The design was fully responsive, with careful attention to typography, layout, and interactive elements to ensure the best user experience across all platforms.

LEARNINGS

Designing the Casa Herrera Seminar website was an enriching experience that expanded my UX/UI design skills. I was able to apply my web design knowledge while working within the specific guidelines of a prestigious brand like Carolina Herrera, ensuring the design aligned with its sophisticated and elegant image.

A key takeaway from this project was the challenge of balancing aesthetics with functionality. I focused on creating a visually appealing interface while ensuring an intuitive user experience, especially on mobile devices, following a "mobile-first" approach. This reinforced my ability to design interfaces that meet both user needs and brand standards.

Testing different user flow options for the registration page was a critical decision. This process highlighted the importance of iteration and usability testing to optimize conversion, ensuring the final design provided an efficient experience.

Collaborating with multidisciplinary teams, including project managers and developers, was another important learning opportunity. The handoff process taught me how crucial it is to provide clear, detailed specifications to ensure seamless implementation, improving both my communication and coordination skills.

The website had a significant impact by:

  • Reducing registration time by 30% compared to last year’s website.
  • Increasing the attendance confirmation rate through a more intuitive registration flow.
  • Enhancing the experience for international users by reducing friction in accessing key information.

If I were to revisit this project, I would have explored more variations of the mobile navigation flow to further enhance the experience for users with diverse needs. Additionally, integrating more interactive elements could have further elevated user engagement, making the experience even more dynamic and memorable.
