THE CHALLENGE

The client, Puig (owner of Carolina Herrera), requested the design of a website for the Casa Herrera Seminar that serves as an exclusive, seamless platform for international professionals attending the event.

The project required a refined balance between aesthetics and functionality, ensuring the site communicates the elegance of the Carolina Herrera brand while providing a highly user-friendly experience. Key goals included:

→ Design a smooth and mobile-optimized registration process website that provides event information and local recomendations for international attendees unfamiliar with the city of Barcelona.

UNDERSTAND

User research

The audience for the Casa Herrera Seminar consists of high-level professionals, such as makeup artists, beauty trainers, and regional managers, many of whom are traveling from abroad. Insights from surveys and interviews with 20 potential attendees highlighted the following key pain points:

  • Mobile usability: Attendees preferred registering on mobile devices but struggled with previous non-responsive event registration websites.
  • Communication barriers: Clear lines of contact with organizers were lacking in past events.
  • Local information: International attendees sought detailed recommendations about the host city, including transportation and accommodation options.

→ Analytics from last year’s event revealed that 68% of attendees accessed the event website via mobile. These insights further reinforced the need for a mobile-first approach in the new website design.

Brand and Market Understanding

The Carolina Herrera brand is synonymous with sophistication, elegance, and exclusivity, so the website’s design had to mirror these values. It was essential to incorporate the brand’s aesthetic guidelines (minimalist elegance, iconic red, and soft pink tones) into the design while making sure the site is intuitive and functional for users.

Casa Herrera Seminar brings together beauty professionals to explore new trends, product launches, and marketing strategies, making it crucial to reflect both the event’s prestige and the evolving needs of the perfume and cosmetics industry

Event context

The Casa Herrera Seminar is an annual event organized by PUIG for Carolina Herrera’s global workforce. It’s a prestigious occasion aimed at enhancing collaboration, showcasing new product launches, and providing specialized training.

The event’s venue, Espai Corberó in Espluges de Llobregat (Barcelona), is renowned for its grand archways, geometric structures, and interplay of light and shadow that create a space that feels both surreal and sophisticated.

Espai Corberó’s clean lines and open spaces provide an opportunity to incorporate subtle architectural references into the website’s aesthetics, offering attendees a preview of the space before they arrive.

DEFINE

User persona

The audience for the Casa Herrera Seminar (and for the web) consists of Carolina Herrera and Puig employees and internal stakeholders from international markets, including regional managers, marketing leads, and other high-responsibility roles.

User stories

As a senior Beauty trainer, I expect a seamless, personalized website experience that reflects the elegance and exclusivity of the Carolina Herrera brand, making me feel like a VIP from the moment I sign up.

Concept: The key to Casa Herrera Seminar

This website is more than just a registration platform; it is the key to entering the exclusive Casa Herrera Seminar. Registering is not simply confirming attendance—it is unlocking a carefully curated, elegant, and refined experience.

Rather than presenting a standard registration form, the website guides users through a step-by-step journey, mirroring the experience of receiving a personal invitation to an exclusive event. Every touchpoint has been designed to evoke the sophistication and prestige of Carolina Herrera:

  • Users will navigate an elegantly structured registration flow, making them feel as if they are receiving a personal invitation to the event.
  • Upon completion, attendees will receive a "Welcome Pass", a visually refined confirmation that reinforces the idea of exclusivity access.
  • Once registered, participants will gain access to their personalized recommendations, ensuring they make the most of their stay in Barcelona.

The website is not just a tool—it is a concierge-style digital experience that welcomes attendees into Casa Herrera Seminar.

Visual Style and Tone of Communication

Casa Herrera Seminar website adopts a minimalist visual style, with sleek illustrations and high-quality imagery, enhancing the user experience without overpowering it.

I created design elements inspired by Espai Corberó’s architecture, with geometric patterns and clean lines paired with soft pinks and the characteristic Carolina Herrera red.

The tone of communication mirrors Carolina Herrera’s globally recognized voice: aspirational, exclusive, and attention to detail. The website’s content uses sophisticated language, ensuring a cohesive, luxury user experience.

IDEATE

Below is a list of basic functionalities for the Casa Herrera Seminar website:

REGISTRATION FORM

  • A step-by-step registration form with real-time feedback and microinteractions to ensure a seamless user experience.

WELCOME PASS

  • A confirmation page that generates a personalized "Welcome Pass" upon successful registration.

LOCAL RECOMENDATIONS

  • After registration, access to curated local insights: maps, attractions, and transportation details tailored for international attendees.

EVENT INFORMATION HUB

  • A centralized section providing clear details on the event schedule, dress code, venue, and other essential information.

COMMUNICATION CHANNELS

  • Easy access to contact details and support options, ensuring users can quickly reach out to event organizers if needed

DATA SECURITY

  • Secure handling of user data and adherence to accessibility standards for an inclusive user experience.

User flow

The primary user flow for the Casa Herrera Seminar website is designed to guide users through their journey seamlessly, with each step focused on providing essential event information and a smooth registration process.

Skecth wireframe

To ensure an intuitive and seamless user experience, I developed sketch wireframes to visualize the entire flow of the website.

The design follows a Mobile Design First metholody, ensuring that all elements are optimized for mobile devices before adapting for larger screens, guarantting that users have a smooth experience across all devices.

ITERATE

With initial concepts in place, I built a Figma prototype and began testing it with real users.

We conducted user testing with a group of 10 potential users to assess their experience with the website across different devices. Testing methods included one-on-one interviews and usability testings.

FINAL DESIGN

After incorporating user feedback and refining the prototype, I prepared the final design and all necessary guidelines, assets, and documentation to ensure a smooth handover.

The design was fully responsive, with careful attention to typography, layout, and interactive elements to ensure the best user experience across all platforms.

LEARNINGS & IMPACT

Designing the Casa Herrera Seminar website was an enriching experience that expanded my UX/UI design skills. I was able to apply my web design knowledge while working within the specific guidelines of a prestigious brand like Carolina Herrera, ensuring the design aligned with its sophisticated and elegant image.

A key takeaway from this project was the challenge of balancing aesthetics with functionality. I focused on creating a visually appealing interface while ensuring an intuitive user experience, especially on mobile devices, following a "mobile-first" approach. This reinforced my ability to design interfaces that meet both user needs and brand standards.

Testing different user flow options for the registration page was a critical decision. This process highlighted the importance of iteration and usability testing to optimize conversion, ensuring the final design provided an efficient experience.

Collaborating with multidisciplinary teams, including project managers and developers, was another important learning opportunity. The handoff process taught me how crucial it is to provide clear, detailed specifications to ensure seamless implementation, improving both my communication and coordination skills.

The website had a significant impact by:

  • Reducing registration time by 30% compared to last year’s website.
  • Increasing the attendance confirmation rate through a more intuitive registration flow.
  • Enhancing the experience for international users by reducing friction in accessing key information.

If I were to revisit this project, I would have explored more variations of the mobile navigation flow to further enhance the experience for users with diverse needs. Additionally, integrating more interactive elements could have further elevated user engagement, making the experience even more dynamic and memorable.
