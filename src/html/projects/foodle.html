FOODLE

an app that provides smart solutions to reduce food waste

Overview

Imagine Foodle as your personal assistant for reducing food waste—making it easier than ever to manage groceries efficiently. Through an intuitive decision tree, Foodle helps young professionals quickly determine the best way to use their food, transforming everyday choices into an engaging experience.

Final Design

Outcomes & Impact

40% Faster Decisions: Usability tests revealed a 40% reduction in decision-making time for managing food at home.

Gamified & Intuitive UX: 85% of users described Foodle as fun and engaging, making the process feel effortless.

Top-Tier Recognition: Foodle achieved a 10/10 score in the final presentation of the UX/UI Master’s at ESDI Higher School of Design.

The Challenge The Solution Design Process Final Design Learnings

THE CHALLENGE

How might we make young Barcelona residents more aware of responsible food consumption?

Young, busy professionals in Barcelona struggle with food management due to fast-paced lifestyles and impulsive grocery shopping. As a result, a significant amount of food is wasted, leading to unnecessary financial and environmental costs. We need to create a user-friendly digital product that would streamline food management, deliver value to users, and encourage sustainable habits.

THE SOLUTION

A mobile app that turns food management into an intuitive and engaging experience. By leveraging an interactive decision tree, Foodle simplifies food-related choices and empowers users to reduce waste efficiently.

Every design decision was driven by three core principles:

Mobile application

A streamlined mobile experience, catering to users who rely on their smartphones for quick decision-making in the kitchen.

Interactive decision tree

A dynamic feature that guides users through food management in a playful and stress-free way. Based on the state of the food, it suggests solutions.

MVP Focus

Prioritized core functionalities rigorously validated through multiple usability tests to guarantee an effective and seamless experience.

DESIGN PROCESS

Through research, ideation, and iterative testing, we crafted a solution that directly addressed user pain points. Together with Lauria Kwan, Malena Guevgeozián, and Anthony Oparah, we ensured every decision was driven by user insights and data-backed validation.

Read about our journey of turning insights into impactful solutions:

1. Understand

In-depth desk research revealed that food waste contributes to 10% of greenhouse gas emissions. Interviews uncovered uncommon pain points like impulse buying and lack of time.

2. Define

Indentified that busy professionals require quick, accessible solutions to minimize waste and optimize grocery management.

3. Ideate

Developed several concepts and refined them into Foodle's MVP - an app that helps users decide how to use their food based on its freshness: good, near expiration, or spoiled.

4. Iterate

Conducted usability testing through guerrilla methods and formal sessions, continuously refining the navigation, interface, and overall functionality.

FINAL DESIGN

After thoroughly refining our design through multiple iterations, we arrived at the final version of Foodle—a user-centered mobile app that effectively addresses food waste management. By consolidating our design assets, guidelines, and documentation, we ensured a smooth handoff to development team.

LEARNINGS

Not falling in love with the first ideas

One of the biggest lessons from Foodle was learning not to get attached to our first ideas. Initially, wour approach was centered on educating users about food waste. However, research revealed that users needed immediate, actionable solutions. Accepting this and pivoting to an interactive decision tree significantly improved engagement and effectiveness.

Miguel, Testing participant: Foodle feels like a game, it's quick to get the information I need, and it doesn’t overwhelm me with too many options, which is exactly what I need after a busy day.

Simplicity an iterative testing are the key to a refined user experience

Design sprints and continuous usability testing were instrumental in refining the user experience. At the beginning, we were tempted to add many features, but user feedback revealed that simplicity was key for our target audience. Prioritizing an MVP helped maintain focus on core functionalities, proving that simplicity enhances usability.

Carles, UX/UI professor You really considered the end user's needs, making the app engaging and functional. Simplicity is the strength of this design: it focuses on what matters, offering a seamless experience.

Deeper research yields greater insights

If revisiting this project, I would refine our research approach by analyzing more specific food waste behaviors and consulting industry experts. Asking the right questions early on unlocks more meaningful, actionable insights.
