CROQUETEO

a fun and sensual identity for a croquette food truck

Overview

Imagine Croqueteo as a street food truck that turns croquettes into a fun, romantic, and irresistible experience. From the moment festival-goers spot the logo to the last crunchy bite, Croqueteo invites them to indulge at every brand touchpoint.

Final Design

Outcomes & Impact

Market Gap Identified: 82% of surveyed young adults had never seen a croquette-specific food truck.

High Engagement: 78% of our target audience (Barcelonians aged between 18 and 35) showed strong interest in trying Croqueteo.

Brand Recognition: A consistent rollout across digital and print ensured strong identity that should resonate in a crowded market.

The Challenge The Solution Design Process Final Design Learnings

THE CHALLENGE

How might we design a food truck concept that stands out in Barcelona’s saturated street food scene?

Barcelona’s younger audiences seek more than just good food—they crave engaging, shareable experiences. With over 500 cultural events a year, food trucks face fierce competition to capture attention. The challenge was to create a brand that embodied a fresh and playful identity, combining taste, aesthetics, and a social media-friendly presence to turn our food truck into a must-visit destination on wheels.

THE SOLUTION

A sensory brand experience that reimagines croquettes as fun, sensual and pleasurable. With vibrant aesthetics, daring flavors, and playful storytelling, Croqueteo creates a multisensory experience, encouraging customers to engage, share, and become part of the brand’s food-loving community.

Every design decision was driven by three core principles:

Festivals focus

We positioned Croqueteo in festivals—dynamic & high-traffic spaces—where an unconventional food concept could stand out and thrive.

Playful & kitsch identity

The name combines the words croqueta and coqueteo, embracing a cheeky, vibrant aesthetic far from traditional gourmet styles.

Abstract flirting symbolism

Instead of clichés like heart icons, we infused the concept of seduction in an unexpected way—drawing inspiration from mythology and Rococó.

DESIGN PROCESS

Together with Ruth García, Montse Grau, and José Morante, we followed an agile and iterative process to ensure that Croqueteo’s identity resonated with real festival-goers.

Read about our journey of turning insights into impactful solutions:

1. Understand

We conducted market research, analyzing competitor food trucks and studying major festivals like Primavera Sound and Sónar to identify trends and opportunities.

2. Define

Croqueteo was designed as a bold, easy to eat at festivals, irresistible and shareable. A vibrant color palette and quirky patterns set the art direction and tone for the brand.

3. Ideate

Starting with hand-drawn sketches, we crafted a brand experience applied consistently across touchpoints, which invited festival-goers to linger, chat, and maybe even flirt.

4. Iterate

We tested Croqueteo’s branding with 15 festival-goers. Their feedback reinforced its playful and flirtatious appeal while guiding refinements for better readability and visual impact.

FINAL DESIGN

We consolidated brand assets, guidelines, and documentation to ensure consistency across all touchpoints—from the logo to packaging, truck design, and promotional materials. The result: a food truck experience that feels immersive, fun, and unforgettable.

LEARNINGS

Balancing playfulness and sophistication

Not every impactful design needs to be obvious. Finding the right balance between fun and refinement was key—small, well-executed details made a stronger impression than literal, predictable choices.

Francesc, Design professor: Croqueteo achieved a brilliant balance between playfulness and sophistication. Her approach shows creative maturity and a great eye for details that truly make an impact.

Consistency builds emotional connection

A visually appealing brand isn’t enough—cohesive design application is what makes it memorable. The way Croqueteo’s identity was executed across touchpoints strengthened its playful personality.

Marina, Testing participant: I loved how everything felt cohesive and well thought out. The identity had personality, but most importantly, it was easy to recognize and remember. The small details made all the difference.

Flexibility and continuous improvement

Testing and real-world application proved invaluable. Seeing how users reacted helped us refine the brand, reinforcing that great design is an evolving process, not a fixed outcome.
